*What's with the name you ask? Isn't it a rather depressing outlook to be a junior developer forever? Well, the idea was that no matter how much you've learned, there always seem to be just as much that you don't know about. As a matter of fact, the more you learn the more you know that you don't know anything. There was this metaphor I once heard. It says that the knowledge of a person is like a circle in a two dimensional plane. The circle's area is the amount of knowledge one possesses, it's circumference is therefore the edge of your ability and represents things you don't know. The smallest size possible for a circle is a dot. A dot is a concept, it's area and circumference are both infinitesimal, which means when you know nothing, you wouldn't even know that you know nothing. Hence only by learning can one even comprehend one's own ignorance. So yes, I'll always be a junior dev in heart, it's the best way to stay vigilant and hungry.
